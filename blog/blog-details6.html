What is Wireframing?

Feb 20, 2023

Wireframing is a visual representation of the basic structure and layout of a user interface. It typically consists of low-fidelity sketches or diagrams that focus on the placement of elements such as buttons, text fields, images, and navigation elements. Wireframes are used to establish the overall flow and functionality of a design before moving on to more detailed prototyping or visual design. They are essential for understanding the user journey, identifying potential usability issues, and ensuring that the design meets the project's goals.
